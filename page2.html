Email marketing involves sending out emails to potential new and repeat customers in order to engage them, generate brand awareness, and increase the chances of a sale.

A common way to build up an email list is offering something of value in exchange for a user submitting their email address, such as

While email marketing can be an excellent tool for finding new customers, it’s also invaluable for maintaining connections with existing customers.

Email marketing, when done right, can be a very effective tactic for spurring action from your customers and potential buyers.

The key is to use the data available to you to segment your lists according to where shoppers are in their car buying process. and then send them an email that is relevant to their goals.

For example, if someone had completed a form on your website expressing interest in a specific make/model but has not yet bought a car,

you could send them an email invite to a special preview of the next model year and entice them to come in to see all the exciting new features in the new model.

OPEN RATE

This metric helps determine how engaged your email audience is.

It’s calculated by dividing the number of emails that were opened by the number of emails that were delivered times 100.

CLICK-THRU RATE

This metric is a good indication of whether the email itself succeeded in piquing the interest of the consumer.

It’s calculated by dividing the number of clicks by the number of total emails sent out times 100.

UNSUBSCRIBE RATE

This metric is crucial, as it determines if people are finding value in the emails being sent to them.

It’s calculated by dividing the number of people who unsubscribe by the total number of emails sent out times 100.

If you have a low open rate, it may be a symptom of weak subject lines or bad send times.

Experiment with more compelling subject lines and what time you send your emails. You can improve your subject lines by:

Using active tense to make the sentence more engaging

Example:

These are the cars people were buying in 2015

People purchased these cars in 2015

Limiting it to between 45 and 65 characters (or 10 words) to keep things clear and concise

Example:

Here’s what experts say you should consider before buying a used car

10 must-ask questions before buying a used car

Creating a curiosity gap (by prompting a question that compels people to click the email to find the answer)

Example:

Don’t buy a car until you’ve considered these 5 questions

Display ads are advertisements that appear on web pages.

Because display ads can be found almost everywhere on the web, they’re a great way to.

generate awareness for your dealership

or

deliver very targeted messages to those car shoppers already engaged with your dealership.

For example, you can run a specific ad targeted at any car shopper who visits a Vehicle Detail Page (VDP) on your website but does not inquire about the car.

Because we know what car the shopper looked at, your ad can include specific messaging about the car to get them to re-engage with you.

If you’re not getting the results you’re looking for, Design Shack has some great tips on how to improve static ads:

Keep things visual and simple.

Graphics should make up a majority of the ad.

The text should be minimal, direct, and clearly convey your main point

Have a clear call-to-action.

Don’t leave readers guessing what to do next;

provide them with a way to learn more, sign up, etc.

Focus on color.

The colors you incorporate will help your ad stand out.

Try a dark background or a plain white background, and make use of “white space” so the main parts of your ad are clear to see.

Also, consider looking at the color scheme of the website that will host your ad and choose colors that contrast with that color scheme.

If you’re noticing that your bounce rate is high, Search Engine Journal has tips on what to optimize to improve results:

Make sure the call-to-action is clear

Your ad may have a good call-to-action, but when someone lands on your site, it should be very obvious what step they should take next.

If it’s not, they’re more likely to back out than move forward.

Optimize your page for mobile

If someone clicks on the ad from their smartphone or tablet, and your site doesn’t cater to these platforms,

they won’t have the patience to navigate.

Minimize site load time.

If your website takes more than a few seconds to load,

you could be losing out on a lot of potential customers who were interested in your call-to-action but not interested in waiting.
