It’s difficult to attribute a lead or sale to a single digital channel since it’s often the culmination of several tactics working together.

This example illustrates why giving all of the credit for a lead to one channel can be misleading.

That being said, getting a top-level view of where leads and dealership traffic are coming from can give a general sense of what is or isn’t working online.

First, you need to decide on how to credit your marketing tactics for driving leads.

In a first-touch attribution model, full credit is given to the lead’s first touch point.

So if a lead clicks on a Facebook post featuring a listing on Craigslist and then visits your website where he or she becomes a lead, Facebook gets credit.

Full credit is given to the touch point where the person became a lead in a last-touch attribution model.

If a lead calls your dealership after receiving an email about a promotion, your email marketing gets credit, even if the lead has also interacted with your dealership on other channels before signing up to receive emails.

A multichannel attribution model takes into account how various marketing channels work together to turn a prospect into a lead. With this approach, credit is given to each touch point that drove the lead to the conversion point.

Let’s say that traffic coming directly from Craigslist has proven to be the highest quality – this traffic has consistently shown a high conversion rate. Meanwhile, traffic coming directly from Facebook has an extremely low conversion rate.

This would be an instance where you would want to assign Craigslist with a higher percentage of credit than Facebook, since you can make the assumption that Craigslist is having a greater impact on conversions.

Unfortunately, you can’t place a tracking device on someone the first time they interact with your dealership online and then follow them into the showroom.

SO HOW CAN YOU GIVE CREDIT TO YOUR DIGITAL EFFORTS FOR DRIVING LEADS AND BRINGING PEOPLE INTO YOUR DEALERSHIP?

Ideally, you’ll want to measure how many showroom visits came from each marketing channel.

Let’s look at a few ways to attribute offline behavior to your online efforts.

Surveys are one of the simplest and most effective ways to give credit to your digital marketing. In addition to collecting this information on customer satisfaction surveys for those who bought a vehicle, ask people who call, email, and visit the dealership how they heard about you.

Collect this information on any forms you require showroom visitors and sales prospects to fill out.

Include a link to a survey in your email correspondence.

Train any employees who answer calls from leads to ask this during the conversation.

Use different phone numbers on digital properties. For example, you can display a different phone number in online listings, email marketing, and on landing pages linked to display ads/paid search to tie back calls from these unique numbers to the respective channel.
