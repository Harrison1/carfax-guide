Search Engine Optimization (or SEO) refers to strategies that help improve your website’s placement in search engine results.

And when customers perform these searches, there’s a high probability they’ll act on what they find.

34% of people who performed a local search on a computer or tablet (and 50% of those on smartphones) went to a store within 24 hours.

If your goals involve increasing brand awareness or getting your dealership out there in front of potential new buyers, SEO is a must. With millions of internet searches happening every single day in your community, it’s important that your dealership isn’t drowned out by the noise.

The higher your keywords rank, the more value you extract from appearing in the results.

For example, the No. 1 position on the first page of the search results will yield a much higher click-thru rate since it is the first result searchers see, thus sending you more potential customers than a keyword that ranks on the third page of the search results.

ORGANIC SEARCH TRAFFIC:

This metric reveals how much of your website’s traffic is coming from people seeing your site appear in the results of their search query (or in their search engine’s results lists), signifying how effective your SEO has been.

Tools like Google Analytics will allow you to determine this metric.

This metric allows you to identify which keywords your site is likely to be found through; it also shows the keywords for which you’re not seeing high enough results to snag the attention of search engine users.

SEMRush is a great tool for checking which keywords you’re ranking for and to monitor increases or decreases in your rankings.

If you’re not ranking for your target keywords, you should consider revamping your SEO strategy.

One of the key factors that search engines use to determine the ranking of a page is the number of other websites that link to your site from their website. The higher the quality and relevancy of the sites linking to your site, the more likely search engines will determine that your site has authority in your topic area.

When it comes to backlinks, quality is more important than quantity, so make that your major consideration.

Characteristics like relevancy and Domain Authority of linking sites are important factors in this determination.

If your site does not have a good profile of backlinks, you should create a link strategy targeted to help increase the number of websites linking to your content.

An effective SEO strategy will gradually increase organic traffic over time. It’s normal for organic traffic to fluctuate from day to day, so don’t become too alarmed by the occasional drop in traffic.

if your traffic drops and doesn’t recover after a few days, it could be due to the following:

A search engine made an update to its algorithm, which negatively impacted your site.

solution:

Check Moz’s Algorithm Change History to see if the date your traffic dropped corresponds with the timing of an algorithm change. This will shed some light on why your site was affected, and from there you can adjust your strategy to align with the update.

You received a manual penalty from a search engine for violating its terms of service or search quality guidelines.

solution:

Check the Manual Actions tab in the Google Search Console for notifications regarding a penalty. Here Google will detail the problem and give recommendations on how to fix it.

However, if you’re not getting the results you’re expecting overall, the first step is to verify that your site’s structure is built to allow search engines to properly “read” and index it.

There are many ways you can adjust the coding structure of your Web pages

to increase the chances search engines are properly interpreting the content:

Make sure every page is linked to – or else search engines won’t have a way of finding it when starting from the homepage.

Use the keyword you’d like to rank for in the <title> tag, at the top of the page’s content, in the meta-description tag, and a couple of times in the body text. Search engines will determine that is what the page is about and the highest priority topic, thus increasing your chances of ranking for that word or term.

EXAMPLE

<title>The Ultimate Used Cars Guide<title>

<meta name=“description” content=“This guide breaks down best practices for consumers looking to buy used cars.”>

[Intro to the body text:]When Americans shop for used cars ...

Cut down on excessive link or keyword usage, if applicable.

If you have too many links, search engines may not index them all.

If you also have too many keyword mentions (to an unnatural extent), search engines may catch on and reduce the value of the keywords.

The best approach is to simply write naturally.

Furthermore, it’s important to keep in mind that HTML is best for indexing purposes – images, Flash, Java, etc. aren’t valued as highly.

For the greatest chance of appearing higher in the search results, you should also research relevant keywords that have less competition. If you discover keyword phrases car buyers might actually be searching for with little competition, take advantage of them.

Even if terms do not receive the most traffic, you should consider them an opportunity.

You’ll be more likely to rank higher (and more quickly) if they’re relevant and there’s less competition.
