Do certain titles call out to you? Do certain types of images capture your attention? Use these insights to craft your posting so it’s more likely to drive interest. When researching which online listing site is right for your dealership, consider the following:

Does the listing site allow people to buy their way to the top of results pages?

This could mean lower-quality results are getting better placement in the results than your listings. Consider sites that prioritize listings by location and relevancy rather than by who pays more.

Does the listing site also include new cars?

Using a site that features both new and used car listings means people visiting the site may be looking for new cars or are on the fence about whether to buy new/used. To reach a more targeted audience, try a listing site that specializes in used cars.

Are there advertisements or other distractions on the page?

If potential customers get sidetracked by banner ads or other calls-to-action, that means they’re less likely to pay attention to your listings. Look for listing sites with straightforward results and few distractions.

If people aren’t viewing your listings, try the following:

Price the car appropriate for the market.

Shoppers may be overlooking your listings in favor of similar listings with better pricing. You can look up used car values free as part of our Advantage Program, which is extremely helpful for setting competitive prices for your listings.

Write more descriptive titles.

In addition to the make and model, mention one of the vehicle's biggest selling points, such as low mileage or leather interior.

Use a clear, well-lit photo of the front of the car as your listing’s main image.

If people are viewing your listings but not clicking through to your site:

Incentivize viewers to click by mentioning a benefit of visiting your site, such as receiving a special offer or being able to watch a video.

Create a sense of urgency with limited-time-only deals or by mentioning the popularity or rarity of the model in the listing.

Furthermore, your listings may be getting bumped down in the search results pages because your competitors paid for premium placement. If this is the case, it can be hard to stand out with your listing alone. Consider allocating some of your online listing budget for premium ad placement, especially for competitive or tough-to-sell models.
