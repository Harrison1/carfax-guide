Display ads are advertisements that appear on web pages.

Because display ads can be found almost everywhere on the web, they’re a great way to.

generate awareness for your dealership

or

deliver very targeted messages to those car shoppers already engaged with your dealership.

For example, you can run a specific ad targeted at any car shopper who visits a Vehicle Detail Page (VDP) on your website but does not inquire about the car.

Because we know what car the shopper looked at, your ad can include specific messaging about the car to get them to re-engage with you.

Cost per thousand, or CPM, is a common metric related to impressions and shows the cost to reach one thousand people via a given advertising outlet or medium.

CLICK-THRU RATE

This metric tells us how many of the people seeing your ad actually take the desired action and click on it. It’s important to test these three variables to determine what the root cause of the poor performance is.

To determine the click-thru rate that’s best for your dealership, you can use a tool like

Google’s Display Benchmarks Tool to compare your results with your competitors’ results.

CONVERSION RATE

This performance metric measures the percentage of ad clicks that result in conversions,

like someone emailing the dealership,

filling out a form,

calling, etc,

To calculate the conversion rate, divide the number of conversions by the number of ad clicks and multiply that number by 100.

This metric is also typically available in your web analytics tool.

If you’re not getting the results you’re looking for, Design Shack has some great tips on how to improve static ads:

Keep things visual and simple.

Graphics should make up a majority of the ad.

The text should be minimal, direct, and clearly convey your main point

Have a clear call-to-action.

Don’t leave readers guessing what to do next;

provide them with a way to learn more, sign up, etc.

Focus on color.

The colors you incorporate will help your ad stand out.

Try a dark background or a plain white background, and make use of “white space” so the main parts of your ad are clear to see.

Also, consider looking at the color scheme of the website that will host your ad and choose colors that contrast with that color scheme.

If you’re noticing that your bounce rate is high, Search Engine Journal has tips on what to optimize to improve results:

Make sure the call-to-action is clear

Your ad may have a good call-to-action, but when someone lands on your site, it should be very obvious what step they should take next.

If it’s not, they’re more likely to back out than move forward.

Optimize your page for mobile

If someone clicks on the ad from their smartphone or tablet, and your site doesn’t cater to these platforms,

they won’t have the patience to navigate.

Minimize site load time.

If your website takes more than a few seconds to load,

you could be losing out on a lot of potential customers who were interested in your call-to-action but not interested in waiting.
