While the shoppers were quick to adapt, not all dealerships have the optimized online presence needed to maximize sales. In addition, If you don’t measure and track your dealership’s digital marketing tactics,
you may be spending your marketing dollars ineffectively while missing opportunities to leverage what’s working.

What’s the best way to create an efficient, cost-effective marketing strategy?

Your digital marketing programs should do three things:

1

Increase brand awareness. Shoppers can’t buy from you if they don’t know about your dealership, which is why awareness is a pertinent part of a marketing plan.

2

Send shoppers to your website. Here, shoppers can view inventory, learn more about your dealership, and (hopefully) convert into leads.

3

Send you quality leads. Whether it’s through email, phone, or in-person connections, effective digital marketing should increase your lead quality and flow.

Based on the above, you’ll want to track the following on a monthly or weekly basis:

What percentage of your website traffic comes from each marketing channel

How many leads come from each marketing channel

And most importantly, how many sales came from each marketing channel

In this guide, you will discover valuable insights for optimizing your digital marketing program and measuring the impact of each tactic to ensure your efforts are generating the highest ROI possible.

One common misconception amongst all marketers – not just those in dealerships – is that raw number of leads is the most important metric for determining the success of a program.

While the total number of leads and even CPL are important metrics to look at, these metrics ignore the quality of those leads. It doesn’t matter if a program is giving you 1,000 leads if none of those shoppers buy a car from your dealership.

That’s why it’s important to look at the quality of the audience and leads when determining the success of your digital marketing programs.

To see this in action, let’s assume there are three different vendors you’re using to list your cars online.

If we were to look only at the total number of leads and cost per lead fields, we would assume that Vendor C was by far our most successful vendor.

VENDOR A VENDOR B VENDOR C
Monthly Cost $1,500 $3,000 $5,000
Number of Leads 10 20 40
Cost per Lead $150 $150 $125
Number of Sales 5 5 5
Cost per Sale $300 $600 $1000

But when we start to look at the quality of those leads - how many of those leads actually bought a car - we start to see that Vendor A and B far outperform Vendor C.

Each of the vendors sold about the same number of cars, but when you consider the cost for each of these vendors we start to see that Vendor A far and away provides us the lowest cost per sale.

This hypothetical example illustrates the importance of tracking lead quality. Without closely examining these numbers, it may appear Vendor C is the most effective of the three vendors; however, it’s really Vendor A that’s providing the greatest return.

While CPL and CPS are both key metrics when determining the success of your marketing efforts, ultimately the most important metric to consider is your return on investment (ROI), which means determining the value of your initiatives’ results and comparing that to the amount you spent to launch them.

When we look at our three hypothetical cases when it comes to ROI, Vendor B sees the highest return on their investment. This example demonstrates that although you may have a low cost per sale for one channel, the sales from that channel may not be as high value as your sales coming from other marketing channels.
