Paid search is a way of pinning your result to the top of the search page.

While many search engines offer this feature, Google AdWords is the most popular and employs the common pay-per-click model, in which you only pay when someone clicks thru the ad to your site.

Bing also offers paid search ads. Although far more people use Google, Bing ads can reach prospects who prefer this search engine.

As explained in the SEO section, search results are a huge part of how dealers can increase awareness of their inventory to potential buyers.

If you’re trying to complement your general search efforts, this is a way to help guarantee your result will be seen.

Google Keyword Planner is an invaluable tool for researching keyword competitiveness on AdWords. The competition rating in Keyword Planner is related to how many advertisers are bidding on that keyword.

Broad keywords tend to be more competitive than specific keywords.

However, high bid prices shouldn’t scare you away from creating ads around certain keywords.

If you’re assessing the success of AdWords campaigns, start by examining your Quality Score. Each of your ad keywords is given a rating from 1 to 10 based on several factors, including click-thru rates, relevance, landing page quality (the page on your site where your ad leads to), and geographic performance. A high Quality Score means Google likes your ads and is rewarding you with high visibility. A strong quality score can lower your cost-per-click (CPC) prices, too. According to WordStream, CPC prices start to decrease once your Quality Score reaches 7 or above.

So what can you do if you need to improve your Quality Score?

Optimize your landing pages to be more user-friendly and relevant to your keyword phrases. Also, make sure that your ads lead to the most relevant page on your site rather than just sending people to your home page.

Perform keyword research to uncover new keyword opportunities. Your ads may be underperforming relative to your competitors, so experimenting with different keywords may lead to greater success.
